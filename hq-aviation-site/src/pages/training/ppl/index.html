Become a Helicopter Pilot

The Private Pilot License (Helicopter) allows you to fly helicopters for private purposes. It's the essential first step whether you want to fly for pleasure, travel, or progress to a commercial career.

Course Structure

What's Involved

Flight Training

Minimum 45 hours flight time including:

  • 25 hours dual instruction
  • 10 hours supervised solo
  • 5 hours solo cross-country
  • 5 hours instrument appreciation
  • Qualifying cross-country (100nm)

Ground School

9 subjects to complete:

  • Air Law
  • Aircraft General Knowledge
  • Flight Performance & Planning
  • Human Performance
  • Meteorology
  • Navigation
  • Operational Procedures
  • Principles of Flight
  • Communications

Before You Start

Prerequisites

The requirements to begin PPL(H) training are straightforward. Most people who want to fly can.

Requirements

  • Minimum age 17 to hold license (can start training earlier)
  • Class 2 Medical Certificate from CAA approved AME
  • English language proficiency (Level 4 minimum)

Medical Certificate

The Class 2 medical is straightforward for most people. It covers basic vision, hearing, and general health. We can recommend approved medical examiners in the area.

Not Sure?

If you're unsure whether you meet the requirements or have any health concerns, we're happy to discuss your situation. Many conditions that you might think would prevent flying are actually fine.
